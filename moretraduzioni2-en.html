Sworn translations

Sworn translations requires that the experts or translators take an oath before an officer of the court, swearing that the work has been done to the best of their ability. It is required by private citizens for certificates, written declarations and diplomas; or by companies, especially in the law sector, for deeds, opinions, contracts, tenders and letters of appointment.

Sworn translations are public acts obtainable through the Registrar at the Courts or the Office of the Justice of the Peace.

The translator must personally take the document to be certified, accompanied by the original text and a statement of oath.

In Italy, translators are not allowed to swear cross-translations (from one foreign language to another): all sworn translations must necessarily be made from Italian.

According to the directives of the Court of Verona, a tax stamp for Euro 14.62 must be applied every four pages (or 100 lines) of text. A tax stamp for Euro 1.81 must be applied to any enclosures to expert reports. No tax stamp is required, however, on trial records for which the court fee has already been paid, and on documents relative to:

- international adoption of minors
- scholarships
- separations and divorces
- introductory records subject to payment
- employment and social security
- primary and secondary school

In some cases, depending on the country of destination and the use for which the document is intended, subsequent to certification, the translated document will have to be legalized by a public authority (Apostilled and Legalized Translations).
